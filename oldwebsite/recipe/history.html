Pozole

History

Pozole originates from Mexico, and it was from the Aztecs and other Mesoamerican tribes. Pozole is a "special occasion" food and was only eaten on special occasions such as holidays. Some historical texts claim that pozole from the Natives was made with sacrificed human flesh! Others claim it was meat from prisoners whose hearts were ripped out in a sacrifice. Luckily, cannibalism isn't practiced today and the meat has been replaced with pork and chicken.
